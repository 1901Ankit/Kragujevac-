Renowned for its esteemed faculty, the university fosters critical thinking and innovation among its students. Beyond academics, the campus buzzes with cultural events and sports activities, promoting a vibrant student life.

The history of Kragujevac

Kragujevac, located in central Serbia, has a diverse history dating back centuries, from its medieval origins to its role as an Ottoman administrative center. In the 19th century, it emerged as a cultural and educational hub, marked by the establishment of Serbia's first modern secondary school. The city's significance continued to grow as it became the capital of the Principality of Serbia in 1818, undergoing rapid urbanization and industrial development. However, it also faced tragedy during World War II with the Kragujevac massacre, leaving a lasting impact on its history. In the post-war era, Kragujevac underwent reconstruction and became a vital industrial and educational center within Yugoslavia. Today, it remains an important city in Serbia, blending its rich past with modern vibrancy.

About Kragujevac

"Kragujevac, nestled in Serbia's heartland, is a city of rich historical significance and modern vitality. As the former capital of Serbia, it played a pivotal role in the nation's cultural and educational development. The city's heritage dates back to medieval times, evolving into a bustling urban center over the centuries. Despite enduring the tragedy of the Kragujevac massacre during World War II, the city rebounded with resilience, becoming a key industrial hub in post-war Yugoslavia. Today, Kragujevac blends its storied past with a thriving present, boasting vibrant cultural attractions, educational institutions, and a dynamic urban landscape. With its blend of history, industry, and culture, Kragujevac stands as a testament to Serbia's enduring spirit and resilience.”

Atmosphere of KRAGUJEVAC

" Kragujevac, nestled in Serbia's Šumadija region, boasts a diverse natural environment. Surrounded by rolling hills, lush forests, and the winding Morava River, it offers picturesque landscapes for outdoor enthusiasts. The region's agricultural richness is evident in its vineyards, orchards, and charming villages. Within the city, parks like Šumarice Memorial Park and Ada Ciganlija provide serene retreats amidst urban life. Efforts towards conservation and sustainable development highlight Kragujevac's commitment to preserving its natural beauty. Overall, Kragujevac's environment blends rural charm with urban amenities, offering residents and visitors alike a refreshing escape into nature.”

LIVING COSTS

"Living costs in Kragujevac are generally affordable compared to many European cities. Rent prices for apartments vary but are typically lower, ranging from €100 to €300 per month. Daily expenses such as groceries and dining out are also reasonably priced, with meals at inexpensive restaurants costing around €3 to €5. Utilities for a small apartment might amount to €50 to €100 monthly. Transportation costs are low, with a single bus ticket priced at around €0.50 to €0.70. Overall, Kragujevac offers a cost-effective living environment, making it appealing for students, professionals, and families."
